# Clarifying SEO Vendor Scope and Responsibilities

**Date:** 2026-04-17 | **Duration:** unknown
**Attendees:** 
**Fathom:** [View Recording](https://fathom.video/share/zyj4DJ1LXMyWkQ_xAeKZP_zLzkp5gKdb)

## Summary
### [Meeting purpose and participant alignment @ 0:00](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=0)

Kevin called the meeting to ensure everyone was on the same page regarding David's roofing business marketing efforts. With David needing to leave in 30 minutes, the team aimed to address questions that Tim and Rod had raised about the engagement and clarify expectations around the work being performed.

### [Local Rankers vendor and initial scope @ 0:47](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=47)

David explained that Local Rankers was recommended by a Colorado contact to help generate leads. The company has been working on multiple channels including SEO, Google Live, and running ads—essentially handling "anything" David needs to move the business forward. This broad mandate became the central point of concern for the meeting.

### [Website technical issues requiring remediation @ 1:32](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=92)

Kevin's team discovered significant website problems during their initial audit: empty pages being indexed by Google, pages that shouldn't be indexed at all, and structural issues. Beyond simple page removal, the team must handle de-indexing and address underlying architecture problems. These foundational fixes are necessary before other SEO efforts can be effective.

### [Core conflict: overlapping vendor responsibilities @ 2:16](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=136)

Kevin identified the critical issue: if Local Rankers is performing both ads and SEO work, the two teams will work against each other and create counterproductive results. While having a separate vendor handle ads is acceptable (Kevin's team doesn't do ads), having two vendors both doing SEO creates confusion about who is responsible for what and prevents effective optimization. Kevin needed clarity on Local Rankers' exact scope.

### [David's frustration with communication gaps @ 3:07](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=187)

David expressed feeling "in the dark" about what's happening with his marketing efforts and investment. He was concerned about the white-label approach and confused about responsibilities. As someone investing significant money, David wanted visibility into how each dollar was being spent and what results it was producing. He acknowledged his tendency to over-communicate and wanted clearer lines of communication.

### [White-label model explained and David's unique position @ 4:30](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=270)

Kevin clarified how the white-label arrangement works: the team does the work and provides reports (month one is setup with no reportable results; month two onward includes findings and path forward). Typically, white-label agencies don't communicate directly with end clients—the agency owner handles that. David's situation is unusual because he is both the agency owner and the client. Kevin explained that if they handled all client interaction themselves, the cost would be $1,500-2,000/month instead of the discounted white-label rate, since they'd be managing all client-facing work.

### [David's responsibilities and hands-off requirement @ 8:08](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=488)

David asked what he could do to help accelerate progress. Kevin explained that David's primary responsibility is to approve content and handle client-facing decisions, but he should not tinker with the website itself—that's what the team is being paid to do. David acknowledged his tendency to fidget with the site when stuck and accepted the directive to leave technical work to the professionals. He emphasized his willingness to help move things faster and mentioned he's only four months into his roofing business in Indianapolis (the 11th largest U.S. city).

### [Website correction work in progress @ 10:14](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=614)

Kevin explained that the team is working through foundational issues because Google won't rank anything well if the site isn't properly set up. Specific work includes: removing empty pages and de-indexing them, checking page structure, and fixing issues like a slider page that was being indexed as a separate page. These are "micro things" individually but collectively time-consuming. Limited communication with David reflects the team's focus on technical corrections rather than approval-requiring tasks.

### [Vendor boundaries and compatibility decision @ 12:16](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=736)

Kevin restated the compatibility issue: Local Rankers can handle ads without conflict, but if they also do SEO work (optimizing Google Business Profile, etc.), the teams become incompatible and must part ways. Kevin emphasized this isn't about quality of work but about preventing conflicting efforts. He asked David to confirm whether he wants Kevin's team or Local Rankers to handle SEO, noting that communication with David as the agency owner is acceptable but the core question is: who does the SEO?

### [Clear definition of SEO responsibilities @ 13:45](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=825)

Tim provided explicit clarity on what constitutes SEO work that cannot be split: website work, Google Business Profile optimization, and backlink building are the three pillars. The clear line in the sand is that ads, PPC, and LSAs belong to Local Rankers; everything else belongs to Kevin's team. Tim emphasized the need for David to get specifics in writing from Local Rankers about exactly what they're doing, to prevent wasted money and effort from overlapping work.

### [Market reality and ranking expectations @ 15:00](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=900)

Tim explained that roofing in the 11th largest city is "not low-hanging fruit" and won't rank overnight. He referenced a client spending $100k/month in Cleveland to contextualize the competitive landscape. David acknowledged understanding Indianapolis is tough but believes there are opportunities in surrounding areas like Shelbyville to generate revenue while building Indianapolis presence. Kevin agreed it's a two-edged sword: high value once ranked, but high barrier to entry.

### [David's business strategy and competitive context @ 18:00](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1080)

David explained he knows the roofing industry well from experience in Florida and Indiana, and he's personally friends with most of his competitors (they attend church together, are in fantasy football leagues). His main bottleneck is being new and unknown compared to established competitors who rely on word-of-mouth. His multi-channel strategy includes: networking meetings, sponsorships, charitable programs (offering free roofs to people with cancer), truck wraps, and ads. He's applying lessons learned from his previous business (DSF) and needs early wins to break even in year one.

### [Commitment and decision framework @ 21:04](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1264)

Kevin reiterated the team's commitment to doing the work they promise. However, he emphasized they don't want to be in a position where work is being done but results aren't moving due to conflicting efforts from another vendor. His clear recommendation: David must decide whether Kevin's team or Local Rankers will handle SEO. There's no middle ground—if Local Rankers touches website, GBP, or backlinks, they must handle all three, and Kevin's team will step back.

### [Website updates and technical progress @ 23:00](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1380)

Tim and Rod reported they've already made visible updates: unpublished all concrete and excavation service pages, fixed the home button to link to the actual home page, and removed references to those services throughout the site. Rod has reworked the footer, headers, and linking structure. They've drafted 16 out of approximately 50 pages. Remaining work includes addressing Divi 5 compatibility issues (the site was built on Divi Alpha), fixing non-functional custom CSS mega menu, ensuring proper cross-linking, handling orphaned pages, and setting up redirects.

### [PPC landing page best practices @ 26:59](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1619)

David asked about timeline for location pages to support ad campaigns. Tim explained that best practice for PPC ads is to send traffic to dedicated landing pages (built on platforms like Go High Level) rather than website location pages, which take time to rank. This approach shouldn't affect SEO work since ads and organic search are separate channels. Tim noted that even large marketing agencies sometimes incorrectly send ads to homepages, but that's not best practice.

### [Shelbyville market analysis and proximity challenges @ 28:45](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1725)

Tim researched Shelbyville (a smaller town David mentioned as an opportunity) and found five roofing companies already ranking there. Google Business Profile rankings are heavily proximity-based, so even competitors located closer to Shelbyville rank higher than David. The strategy is to focus on organic ranking first, then work toward GBP ranking, acknowledging that proximity is a significant factor. Tim also noted that search results show Yelp, BBB, Cochran, listicles, and Angie's List competing for visibility, and discovered David has a competitor with an almost identical name (317 Roofing and Restorations).

### [Action items and closing with David @ 30:04](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1804)

Kevin asked David to contact Local Rankers and get their specific responsibilities in writing, then report back to the team. Kevin confirmed they'd provided sufficient information and invited David to reach out if he had additional questions. David agreed and ended the call.

### [Post-call team analysis and market insights @ 30:37](https://fathom.video/calls/641878045?tab=summary&timestamp=1837)

After David left, Kevin, Tim, and Rod discussed the call. Tim felt they were initially "beating around the bush" before clarifying the three SEO pillars, which helped focus the conversation. The team discussed whether David fully understood the market challenges: in Shelbyville, six companies rank by proximity, and David's location is further away, making GBP ranking particularly difficult. They agreed David's best approach is to do excellent work, build reputation through direct outreach, and recognize that organic ranking takes time. Tim noted the complexity of the competitive landscape with similar company names and multiple ranking factors beyond SEO. Kevin acknowledged they've laid the groundwork for moving forward.
